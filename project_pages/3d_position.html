Project Background & Description

Falls among elderly individuals are a critical issue, particularly in care facilities. In Singapore, where one in four citizens will be aged 65 or older by 2030, over 25% of individuals aged 65 and above experience a fall each year. These falls can lead to severe injuries, and in some cases, falls account for 40% of injury-related deaths among older adults.

This project aims to address this problem by using ArUco markers and OpenCV to monitor elderly residents' movements in real-time, detecting potential falls and notifying caregivers immediately. It also includes door motion detection for bathroom monitoring, alerting caregivers if a resident has been inside for too long. This approach is designed to provide prompt assistance in case of falls, and a differentiated alarm mechanism is included to minimize false alerts, ensuring that notifications are accurate and timely.
