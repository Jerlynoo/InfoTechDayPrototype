Project Background & Description

Three-dimensional (3D) building models are essential for urban heat island effect, light pollution, and solar energy potential studies etc. Such studies depend on detailed data of building surfaces such as material, texture, and albedo. However, current research methods to gather building surfaces data are highly restricted, costly and/or limited. For eg, traditional surveys are resource intensive and satellite imagery only captures 2D roof details. Thus, a new approach is needed to integrate multi-scale inputs of detailed and contextual information to create accurate, useful and accessible 3D models.

This research introduces a deep learning framework which uses the AdvUNet3+ model to segment building façade materials from street view images (SVIs) and project them onto 3D models, thus providing an enriched spatial representation of urban settings. The project contributes: (1) the AdvUNet3+ model, which integrates multi-scale SVIs and uses advanced modules to capture both detail and context effectively; (2) an innovative segmentation method with high performance metrics, trained on a dataset of 800 labeled SVIs in central Singapore; and (3) a scalable process to integrate segmented materials with 3D models, demonstrated on over 24,000 SVIs.

Indeed, this framework overcomes the challenges of cost and accessibility through traditional methods. Employment. It also enriches the 3D building models with detailed façade information. It is highly adaptable and generalizable, supporting applications in solar potential assessment, heat distribution analysis, and urban planning across cities.
