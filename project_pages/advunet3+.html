Project Background & Description

Three-dimensional (3D) building models are essential for studying urban phenomena like the urban heat island effect, light pollution, and solar energy potential. Detailed data on building surfaces—such as materials, textures, and albedos—are critical for accurate findings in these studies. However, current methods to obtain such data are either highly restricted, costly, or limited in scope, as traditional surveys are resource-intensive, and satellite imagery only captures 2D roof details. Given these limitations, a new approach that integrates multi-scale inputs and emphasizes detailed and contextual information is necessary to make 3D models more accurate and accessible.

In response, my research introduces a deep learning framework utilizing the AdvUNet3+ model to segment building façade materials from street view images (SVIs) and project them onto 3D models, thus providing an enriched spatial representation of urban settings. The project contributes: (1) the AdvUNet3+ model, which integrates multi-scale SVIs and uses advanced modules to capture both detail and context effectively; (2) an innovative segmentation method with high performance metrics, trained on a dataset of 800 labeled SVIs in central Singapore; and (3) a scalable process to integrate segmented materials with 3D models, demonstrated on over 24,000 SVIs. This framework not only overcomes the challenges of cost and accessibility associated with traditional methods but also enriches 3D building models with spatially detailed façade information. It is highly adaptable and generalizable, supporting applications in solar potential assessment, heat distribution analysis, and urban planning across various cities.
