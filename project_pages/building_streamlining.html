Project Background & Description

​ What is your project about? ​ My project involves developing a custom driver for the Leishen C16 LiDAR to enhance how it communicates with our own robot's internal software by eliminating the need for ROS (Robot Operating System) Instantaneous reaction times are critical, especially when it encounters dynamic moving objects or people, hench cutting the ROS cord is much needed to make robots smarter.

What is Leishen C16 LiDAR? A LiDAR short for Light Detection and Ranging is a technology that uses lasers to measure distances by calculating the time light takes to reflect back from the object detected.​ Leishen C16 LiDAR is a 16-channel LiDAR sensor our company uses for autonomous robot navigation. ​

What problem does it solve? ​ Currently, most LiDARs rely on ROS for integration. While versatile, ROS can introduce delays and complexity. By removing the need for ROS, my driver cuts off unnecessary delays and reduces latency, allowing the robot to process the data collected from the LiDAR more efficiently. This improvement ensures quicker response time, enabling the robot to navigate around its surrounding with greater accuracy and speed. Which is highly crucial for product preparation and handling of dynamic environments​
