Project Background & Description

In Singapore, the role of caregiving is often performed by family members and foreign domestic helpers. Coupled with other responsibilities and language difficulties, managing the care recipients’ or patients’ medical conditions and medications can be challenging.​ ​

A survey administered on 24 caregivers found that a number of caregivers had difficulties in medication administration and understanding their elderly care recipient’s complaints when the latter spoke in languages other than English (e.g., Mandarin and Hokkien). Hampered by communication challenges, caregivers are 5 times more likely to experience caregiver burden.​ ​

GRACE (Guided Resources, Assistance and Communication for Empowered Care) app is an interactive mobile application that supports caregivers in the provision of medications to patients. The app’s core functionalities encompass storing medication information, scanning medication labels to text, translating medication information and FAQs about medications. This application aims to help caregivers improve medication adherence and management of the patient. With medication information accessible to both caregivers and patients, GRACE empowers both parties to actively manage the patient’s health and fosters a more collaborative and patient-centric approach within the healthcare ecosystem.​ ​​

The majority of the local caregiver apps do not offer language translation features. Among those that do, none offers any translation feature for medication labels. Hence, the GRACE app aims to cater to caregivers of diverse nationalities by allowing for translation of languages commonly used by our community, such as Mandarin, Malay, Tamil, Indonesian or Tagalog.
