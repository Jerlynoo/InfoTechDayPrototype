Project Background & Description

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) affects millions worldwide, yet misconceptions and a lack of awareness continue to pose barriers for individuals on the spectrum. The public may struggle to understand the sensory, social, and emotional challenges faced by people with autism, leading to stigma and inadequate support. To address this gap, the interactive educational SEN virtual reality (VR) game was developed. ​

The game aims to educate, foster empathy, and promote meaningful action, empowering players to create a more inclusive society for individuals with autism. Set in the future, the game immerses players in scenarios that highlight struggles faced by people with autism. It also incorporates strategies for players to understand and empathise with them, fostering inclusivity. ​

Key features of the game include VR, where immersive gameplay allows players navigate real-life challenges faced by those with autism; and visual and auditory effects simulate sensory sensitives (e.g. breathing detection. Through this game, it is hoped that players can learn about the struggles faced by people with autism in an engaging and meaningful way.​
